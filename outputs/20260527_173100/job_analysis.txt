### Job Analysis Report: Data Scientist (Emerging Markets)
**Agency:** Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC)  
**Location:** Washington, D.C.  
**Closing Date:** May 29, 2026

---

#### 1. Required Technical Skills
*   **Programming:** Proficiency in Python, R, or SAS for data manipulation and statistical analysis.
*   **Data Analysis & Modeling:** Advanced statistical modeling, machine learning, econometric analysis, and quantitative research methods.
*   **Data Visualization:** Experience with tools such as Tableau, Power BI, or Python-based libraries (Matplotlib, Seaborn) to communicate findings.
*   **Database Management:** SQL for complex querying and large dataset management.
*   **Domain Knowledge:** Understanding of financial markets, commodity derivatives, and emerging market structures.

#### 2. Soft Skills and Qualities
*   **Analytical Thinking:** Ability to decompose complex financial datasets into actionable intelligence.
*   **Communication:** Excellent written and verbal communication skills for presenting findings to non-technical stakeholders and leadership.
*   **Adaptability:** Capacity to learn new regulatory frameworks and market dynamics rapidly.
*   **Collaboration:** Strong interpersonal skills for working in cross-functional teams (legal, policy, and enforcement).
*   **Intellectual Curiosity:** A proactive approach to investigating market anomalies and emerging trends.

#### 3. Experience Level and Years Required
*   **Grade Level:** Generally aligned with GS-13/14 (equivalent to mid-to-senior level).
*   **Experience:** 3–5+ years of progressive professional experience in data science, economics, or quantitative analysis within a financial or regulatory environment. 
*   **Education:** A degree in Statistics, Economics, Mathematics, Computer Science, or a related quantitative field is typically required to qualify for federal Data Scientist series (1560).

#### 4. Key Responsibilities
*   **Market Monitoring:** Identifying and analyzing patterns in trading data to detect potential market abuse or systemic risks.
*   **Model Development:** Building, testing, and implementing predictive models to support policy decisions and regulatory oversight.
*   **Policy Support:** Providing data-driven insights to inform the rulemaking process for emerging financial technologies and markets.
*   **Technical Reporting:** Authoring detailed technical memos and research papers for CFTC leadership and regulatory committees.
*   **Collaboration:** Partnering with economists and lawyers to investigate complex derivatives trading activities.

#### 5. Top 5 Resume Keywords
1.  **Quantitative Analysis**
2.  **Statistical Modeling**
3.  **Data Visualization**
4.  **Financial Derivatives** (or Commodity Markets)
5.  **Regulatory Oversight**

#### 6. What the Agency Values Most
*   **Market Integrity:** The CFTC prioritizes candidates who demonstrate a commitment to protecting the integrity of U.S. derivatives markets.
*   **Evidence-Based Decision Making:** They value candidates who can bridge the gap between "raw data" and "regulatory policy." The agency looks for individuals who do not just report numbers, but explain the *implications* of those numbers on market participants.
*   **Technical Rigor:** Because this role involves high-stakes financial regulation, the agency places a premium on candidates who demonstrate precision, reproducibility in their coding/models, and a deep understanding of the regulatory environment.
*   **Proactivity:** They value "emerging markets" expertise—specifically, the ability to anticipate how new technologies (like DeFi or new fintech products) will impact the existing regulatory landscape.
